About This Trek

The Dayara Bugyal trek is a short yet incredibly scenic journey in the Garhwal Himalayas, ideal for those looking to experience high-altitude meadows without extreme difficulty. The trail begins through dense forests of oak and rhododendron, gradually opening up into expansive alpine grasslands known as bugyals.

As you gain elevation, the views become wider and more dramatic, revealing peaks like Bandarpoonch, Draupadi Ka Danda, and Gangotri range. Camps are set in vast open meadows, offering stunning sunsets and star-filled skies. The trek combines gentle ascents with rewarding views, making it perfect for beginners and families while still being visually spectacular.

In August and September, the meadows are a riot of wildflowers. By October, the grasslands turn golden and you get some of the clearest mountain views of the year. Either window is extraordinary.

Why This Trek With Karan

Small groups, proper camping setup, good food, and real attention to each person's experience.

Who Is This For

  • First-time trekkers and those new to the Himalayas
  • Families with older children (12+)
  • Groups wanting a long weekend or short escape into the mountains
  • Anyone looking for spectacular alpine landscapes without the physical demands of a longer high-altitude trek

No prior trekking experience required. A reasonable level of fitness (comfortable walking 4–5 hours) is sufficient.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day 1 Drive to Raithal 2,286m ▾
Drive to the base village of Raithal from Dehradun or Rishikesh, passing through the Bhagirathi valley with views of the Himalayas unfolding along the route. Evening kit check and briefing. ~8 hrs drive from Delhi.
Day 2 Raithal to Gui Campsite 3,050m ▾
Trek begins through forests of oak and rhododendron — classic Garhwal mountain forest. The trail is well-defined and moderate in gradient. Gui is a small campsite set at the forest edge, with the meadows beginning just above. Walk: ~4–5 hrs.
Day 3 Gui to Dayara Bugyal – return to Gui 3,650m ▾
The meadow day. Ascend into the vast open expanse of Dayara Bugyal — grasslands stretching in every direction, with Bandarpoonch (6,316m), Draupadi Ka Danda, and the Gangotri peaks as backdrop. The scale and silence are extraordinary. Explore the upper meadows, then descend back to Gui for the night. Walk: ~6 hrs total.
Day 4 Gui to Raithal – Drive back 2,286m ▾
Descend back through the forest to Raithal. Enjoy a final Himalayan morning before the drive out. Trek ends.
